Ronald J Hirschauer

Front end web developer working toward full-stack designation. Skilled with CSS, experience with React. Javascript experience. Comfortable with command line, VIM and work with CI/CD.

About Me

I was born in a Chicago suburb, where I lived until I was 11. My father was transferred to the east coast and my family moved to western Connecticut. While there, I completed my public education, received my bachelors from Western Connecticut State University, and a masters degree from Fordham University.

In my late twenties, I moved to Providence, Rhode Island where I have remained.

About My Work

My employment experience has been vast and varied. I began my first career as a clinical social worker specializing in mental health care. After many years of experience, I wanted to do something different. My parents, especially my father, inspired me to appreciate history; having a passion for historic architecture historic restoration, I obtained a real estate license and specialized in sale of historic residential property.

When the real estate market crashed in the early 2000's, a real estate sales job was no longer sustainable. I took a part-time job in retail to make ends meet. Eventually, I was offered full time employment and became a customer service manager, and remained in this position for a number of years. Given the lack of mobility and relatively low pay, I moved on.

Throughout all of my employment experience I had a proficiency for working with computers, primarily focused on using software to enhance my job performance. I developed data-bases, tracked data, identify trends, and used the data to create efficient work-flows, and ensure compliance with performance objectives.

After the sale of personal real estate, I had an opportunity to make another career change and persued my interest in working with computers. I left my job, lived off my savings, and began to educate myself about coding and web-design. I decided to focus on becoming a full stack web-developer.

It was a difficult road, but not for obvious reasons. After several months of success with online tutorials (there are several excellent ones that are free of charge, and others of equal quality at low cost), I found it difficult to pull all that I had learned together. I found an online resource of experts with whom I consulted to further my knowledge.

After a few disappointing experiences, I was very fortunate. I found a teacher, a mentor who opened the door for me. He was highly skilled as an instructor. I was fortunate to have connected with him. What I learned from him was invaluable. He facilitated my learning. I will forever be grateful for what I learned. His knowledge and experience are exceptional. Our work together lead to the creation of several projects. Each incorporating technology as I learned it. I was also encouraged to utilize my creativity. My mentor, always aware of the most current technological developments, encouraged me to use them. I was able to do so, and incorporated some of what I learned into a number of relatively simple projects that helped me to learned the foundational components of web-design. I also acquired basic knowledge of back-end coding.

In addition, I learned about many online resources for web-development - always at the edge of technological development. Some of the technology and resources he shared were utilized in creating my projects.
